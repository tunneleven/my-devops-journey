# AWS Acceptable Use Policy

> AWS AUP = The "Don't Do This" list - rules for what you CANNOT do on AWS

---

## What Is It?

The **AWS Acceptable Use Policy (AUP)** defines prohibited activities on AWS services. If you violate it, AWS can **suspend or terminate** your account.

## ğŸ“ Exam Practice Questions

### Question 1
**Which policy describes prohibited uses of AWS services?**

A) AWS Fair Use Policy  
B) AWS Acceptable Use Policy  
C) AWS Service Level Agreement  
D) AWS Trusted Advisor  

<details><summary>Answer</summary>
**B) AWS Acceptable Use Policy** - The AUP defines what activities are prohibited on AWS.
</details>

---

### Question 2
**What happens if you violate the AWS Acceptable Use Policy?**

A) You receive a warning only  
B) AWS may suspend or terminate your account  
C) Nothing, it's just a guideline  
D) You pay a fine  

<details><summary>Answer</summary>
**B) AWS may suspend or terminate your account** - Violations can result in service suspension or account termination.
</details>

---

### Question 3
**Which of the following is PROHIBITED by the AWS Acceptable Use Policy?**

A) Running production workloads  
B) Using multiple AWS accounts  
C) Sending unsolicited bulk emails (spam)  
D) Hosting websites  

<details><summary>Answer</summary>
**C) Sending unsolicited bulk emails (spam)** - Spam is explicitly prohibited by the AUP.
</details>
